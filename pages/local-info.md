## Welcome to Exeter

Exeter is a vibrant cathedral city in Devon, South West England. With a rich history dating back to Roman times, Exeter offers visitors a unique blend of heritage, culture, and modern amenities. The city serves as a gateway to the beautiful Devon countryside, Dartmoor National Park, and the stunning Jurassic Coast.

## Getting to Exeter

### By Air

**Exeter Airport (EXT)**
- Located 5 miles east of the city centre
- Domestic flights from various UK airports
- Limited European connections
- Bus and taxi services to city centre

**Bristol Airport (BRS)**
- Major regional airport, approximately 80 miles from Exeter
- Wide range of UK and European flights

**London Airports**
- Heathrow (LHR), Gatwick (LGW), and Stansted (STN)
- Train connections to Exeter (see below)

### By Train

Exeter has two main railway stations:

**Exeter St Davids**
- Main intercity station
- Direct services from London Paddington (approximately 2 hours)
- Connections to Bristol, Birmingham, and the North
- Closest station to the university campus

**Exeter Central**
- City centre location
- Local and regional services

**Useful Links:**
- [National Rail](https://www.nationalrail.co.uk) - Train times and tickets
- [GWR](https://www.gwr.com) - Main operator for London-Exeter route

### By Car

Exeter is well-connected by road:

- **M5 Motorway** - From Bristol and the North
- **A30/A303** - From London and the South East
- **Approximate driving times:**
  - London: 3.5-4 hours
  - Bristol: 1.5 hours
  - Birmingham: 2.5 hours

**Parking**: City centre parking is limited and can be expensive. We recommend using public transport to the campus.

### By Coach

**National Express** operates regular services to Exeter from major UK cities.
- [nationalexpress.com](https://www.nationalexpress.com)

## About Exeter

### History

Exeter's history spans nearly 2,000 years:

- **Roman era** - Founded as Isca Dumnoniorum
- **Medieval period** - Major wool trading centre
- **Cathedral** - Construction began in 1112, one of England's finest Gothic cathedrals
- **World War II** - Significant bombing damage, much rebuilt

### Weather in April

April in Exeter typically offers mild spring weather:

- **Average high**: 13-15°C (55-59°F)
- **Average low**: 5-7°C (41-45°F)
- **Rainfall**: Moderate; bring a waterproof jacket
- **Daylight**: Long days with sunset around 8 PM

**Recommendation**: Dress in layers and bring waterproof clothing. UK weather can be unpredictable!

## Attractions

### Must-See

**[Exeter Cathedral](https://www.exeter-cathedral.org.uk/)**
- Stunning Gothic architecture
- Longest uninterrupted vaulted ceiling in England
- Astronomical clock from the 15th century

**[Underground Passages](https://exeter.gov.uk/leisure-and-culture/our-attractions/underground-passages/)**
- Medieval passages beneath the city
- Guided tours available

**[Royal Albert Memorial Museum (RAMM)](https://rammuseum.org.uk/)**
- Free entry
- Natural history, world cultures, and local history

**[Exeter Quayside](https://www.visitexeter.com/things-to-do/exeter-quayside-p1215273)**
- Historic waterfront area
- Cafes, pubs, and antique shops
- Canal walks and boat hire

### Day Trips

**[Dartmoor National Park](https://www.dartmoor.gov.uk/)**
- Dramatic moorland landscapes
- Hiking, wildlife, and historic sites

**[Jurassic Coast](https://jurassiccoast.org/)**
- UNESCO World Heritage Site
- Spectacular coastal cliffs and beaches

**[Torbay](https://www.englishriviera.co.uk/)**
- English Riviera beaches
- Torquay, Paignton, and Brixham

## Practical Information

### Currency

- **British Pound Sterling (GBP / £)**
- Card payment is standard throughout the UK. Most places are card-only, including the university campus
- ATMs are available in the city centre

### Electricity

- **230V, 50Hz**
- **UK three-pin plug (Type G)**
- Bring adapters if needed

### Mobile Phones

- UK uses GSM 900/1800 and 3G/4G/5G networks
- EU roaming charges may apply post-Brexit
- Check with your provider before travelling

### Emergency Services

- **Emergency**: 999 or 112
- **Non-emergency police**: 101
- **NHS (health) advice**: 111
