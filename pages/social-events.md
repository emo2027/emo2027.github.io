## Social Programme

EMO 2027 offers an exciting social programme designed to foster networking, collaboration, and enjoyment outside of the technical sessions. All social events provide opportunities to explore Exeter's charm while connecting with fellow researchers.

## Boat Tour on the Exe Estuary

<p class="event-datetime"><strong>Tuesday, April 6, 2027 | Evening (TBC)</strong></p>

<p class="event-datetime"><strong>Departure: Exeter Quay</strong></p>

Join us for a private charter boat tour along the River Exe Estuary. We plan to use Stuart Line Cruises (Pride of Exmouth), subject to final confirmation.

### Tour Highlights

- Scenic journey along the River Exe
- Views of historic Topsham and the estuary
- Opportunity to spot birds and marine wildlife
- Expert commentary on local history and ecology
- Refreshments on board (TBC)

### Timing

Detailed timings will be announced closer to the conference.

### Practical Information

- **Duration**: Approximately 2.5 hours on the water (TBC)
- **Dress code**: Casual, comfortable clothes; layers recommended
- **Weather**: Tour operates in most weather conditions; waterproofs advised
- **Mobility**: Please inform us of any accessibility requirements when booking
- **Capacity**: Up to 150 (TBC)

## Exeter Exploration

### Self-Guided Options

For those wanting to explore Exeter independently, we recommend:

**Historic Exeter Walking Tour**
- Free self-guided routes available
- Start at the Cathedral and explore the historic centre

**Exeter Quayside**
- Historic waterfront area
- Cafes, antique shops, and canal walks
- Bike hire available

**Royal Albert Memorial Museum**
- Free entry
- Excellent collections on world cultures and local history

### Guided Tours

Visit Exeter offers various guided tours. Check [visitexeter.com](https://www.visitexeter.com) for options.
